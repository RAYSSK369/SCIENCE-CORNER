MISSION TO MARS

Why NASA Wants Humans on Mars

  1. Understand Planetary Evolution
     🪐 Studying Mars helps us learn how rocky planets (like Earth) form and change over time.
  2. Prepare for De
  3. Search for Life
     🔍 Mars may have had water in the past, which means it could have supported life. Humans can help search for signs of ancient or even present life more effectively than robots.
  4. ep Space Exploration
     🌌 Going to Mars helps develop the tech, skills, and knowledge needed for future missions even deeper into space.
  5. Protect Earth’s Future
     🌍 Learning how to live on another planet can help humanity survive long-term threats like asteroid impacts or climate disasters.
  6. Inspire Innovation and Exploration
     💡 Big missions like this drive scientific and technological breakthroughs—and inspire people around the world.

    HUMANS ESTABLISHING A PRESENCE ON MARS AND EUROPA PRESENT VASTLY DIFERENT POSSIBILITIES AND CHALLENGES

    Mars:

     

     

     

    Possibilities:

    • Resource Utilization: Mars has resources like water ice, carbon dioxide (which can be converted to oxygen and fuel), and regolith (soil) that could be used for construction and life support.
    • Scientific Discovery: Mars offers the potential to study planetary evolution, search for evidence of past or present microbial life, and understand geological processes different from Earth's.
    • Planetary Backup: Establishing a self-sustaining colony on Mars could serve as a "backup" for humanity in case of a catastrophic event on Earth.
    • Technological Advancement: The challenges of Martian colonization would drive innovation in various fields like space travel, robotics, closed-loop life support systems, and materials science.
    • Expansion of Human Civilization: Mars represents a new frontier for human exploration and settlement, potentially leading to a multi-planetary species.
    • Longer Days: A Martian day (sol) is only about 40 minutes longer than an Earth day, which could make adapting easier for human circadian rhythms.
    • Seasons: Mars has seasons similar to Earth, although they are about twice as long
